Kung Fu Hustle: A Review of the Whole Movie Except the Last 1 Hour, 31 Minutes, and 25 Seconds

The scene is set: a ruthless gang mercilessly slaughtered by an even more ruthless gang. The more ruthless gang is not only merciless, but also runs an illegal (I assume) gambling ring, and does choreographed dances to celebrate after a slaughter. Doesn't get more ruthless than a choreographed dance after chopping someone's leg off with a thrown axe and then shooting a woman in the back. The police are useless and are powerless to fight back.

Meanwhile, the only hope to rid the city of this menace comes from the poorest part of town, where manual labor keeps him resilient and strong as shit. Despite being poor, there's a sense of community. People help and protect each other here.

Intentional shot choices quickly convey the vibe. A montage of black and white photos, meaning this must be based on real events. Action that tells a story instead of just being "cool". A story of good vs evil, money vs community, and doing whatever it takes to protect the people you love against insurmountable odds.
