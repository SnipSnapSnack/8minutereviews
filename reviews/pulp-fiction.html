Pulp Fiction

~ 1994 · Directed by Quentin Tarantino · Rated R ~

Reviewed by VHSVince — 07/14/1996

★★★★★ (5 out of 5 milkshakes)

The Skinny

Three crime stories in L.A. all bleed into each other, told out of order. Two hitmen quote scripture before they ice you, a boxer doesn't take a dive, and a gangster's wife really REALLY wants a five dollar milkshake. Somehow this all adds up to one of the best movies of the decade.

Why It Rules

  • The dialogue. People talk about foot massages, fast food, and Madonna lyrics like real human beings, except funnier and slightly more likely to be murdered.
  • John Travolta got his career back — we should all be so lucky.
  • Samuel L. Jackson's monologue in the diner. Watch it. Watch it again. Watch it a third time. Tell your friends.
  • The non-linear timeline means you have to actually PAY ATTENTION at a movie, which I know is bogus, but trust me.

The Not-So-Rad

If you don't like swearing, this is not the flick for you. There is also a needle scene I have personally decided does not exist.

"They call it a Royale with cheese." — my new go-to icebreaker.

The Verdict

Five out of five. Quentin Tarantino has officially earned the right to make whatever he wants forever. Rent it tonight, and rewind it before returning it — we're cool like that here at 8 Minute Reviews.
