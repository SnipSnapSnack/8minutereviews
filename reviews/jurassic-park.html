Jurassic Park

~ 1993 · Directed by Steven Spielberg · Rated PG-13 ~

Reviewed by RaptorRandy — 06/12/1995

★★★★☆ (4 out of 5 amber-trapped mosquitoes)

The Skinny

A billionaire clones dinosaurs from prehistoric mosquito blood and builds a theme park around them. A small group of scientists is invited to sign off on the place. Everything goes wrong in the exact way Jeff Goldblum predicted it would, while explaining chaos theory in the most flirtatious way possible.

Why It Rules

  • The T-Rex paddock scene. The water ripples. The fence falls. You will not breathe for nine minutes.
  • The raptors in the kitchen. You will never trust a stainless steel countertop again.
  • The CGI is genuinely jaw-dropping. We have officially arrived in The Future, folks.
  • "Hold onto your butts." — Samuel L. Jackson, lighting a cigarette, becoming a meme decades early.

The Not-So-Rad

The kids are kind of annoying, but to be fair, they are being chased by velociraptors, so I'll allow it.

"Your scientists were so preoccupied with whether or not they could, they didn't stop to think if they should." — my new excuse for not doing the dishes.

The Verdict

Four out of five. Half a star off only because the sequel is gonna have a lot to live up to. This is the summer blockbuster all other summer blockbusters wish they were.
