Titanic

~ 1997 · Directed by James Cameron · Rated PG-13 ~

[ Home ] [ Pulp Fiction ] [ Jurassic Park ] [ The Matrix ] [ Titanic ] [ About ]

Reviewed by RoseyRiveter — 12/22/1997

★★★★☆ (4 out of 5 giant blue diamonds)

The Skinny

You know the boat. You know the iceberg. You know the door. James Cameron spends 3 hours and 14 minutes between those plot points to tell a love story between a poor sketch artist (Leo) and an engaged rich girl (Kate), and then he sinks an entire luxury liner on top of it.

Why It Rules

  • The ship sinking sequence is one of the most jaw-dropping things ever filmed. You can feel the cold.
  • Kate Winslet is acting CIRCLES around everyone, and Leo is right there with her.
  • Celine Dion. The song. THE song. You know the one. Yes, the one in your head right now.
  • James Cameron built a near-full-scale Titanic for this. Hollywood is officially out of its mind, and I'm here for it.

The Not-So-Rad

The door, Rose. THE DOOR. Look, I have measured it in my head, I have measured it on graph paper, there was room. There was room. I will be in my bunk.

"I'll never let go, Jack." Then she lets go. SHE LETS GO.

The Verdict

Four out of five. Bring tissues. Bring more tissues than you think you need. Then bring an extra box for the friend you dragged with you.
